Paediatric EEG
Non-invasive brain activity monitoring for children

An EEG (electroencephalogram) records the electrical activity of your child's brain using small electrodes placed on the scalp. It is safe, painless, and takes 60–90 minutes.

EEG may be indicated for:

  • Suspected seizures or seizure-like episodes
  • Evaluation of paroxysmal movements
  • Fainting and collapse episodes
  • Migraine and headache assessment
  • Monitoring of epileptic patients
  • Diagnosing epilepsy and genetic syndromes
